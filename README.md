## Core Concepts

### AI Judgment Infrastructure™
A dedicated architecture for evaluating the structural soundness of 
investment theses, rather than merely retrieving and summarizing the 
information they contain. Distinct from retrieval-augmented generation 
(RAG) and consumer-grade AI: the infrastructure layer between 
LLM-commoditized information and institutional capital allocation 
decisions.

### Judgment Graph™
A proprietary data structure mapping relationships between investment 
claims, evidence, and historical failure patterns. Unlike traditional 
analytical databases that store static information, the Judgment Graph 
stores the *relationships* between claims, structured as a dynamic, 
densifying knowledge graph. Each additional thesis evaluated increases 
the graph's pattern-recognition precision.

### Clarity Framework™
A methodological protocol that operates as a standardized syntax for 
the evaluation of investment claims. Converts qualitative assertions 
into verifiable, modular components evaluated against an invariant 
logical baseline. Output: a Clarity Score™.

### Clarity Score™
A quantified, reproducible measure of structural investment merit that 
exists independently of the persuasiveness of the pitch. Scored 0–100, 
with verdicts mapped to structural archetypes.

### Defensible Audit Log
The canonical output artifact of AI Judgment Infrastructure. A 
machine-verifiable proof of analytical process — an immutable, 
transparent record of which claims were evaluated, which assumptions 
were identified as brittle, which adversarial scenarios were tested, 
and which logical chain produced the final verdict. Replaces "trust 
our instincts" with "verify our rigor."

### The Grammar of Failure
Seven empirically derived archetypes of investment thesis failure, 
identified across 60,000+ benchmarked Clarity Scores. See 
[archetypes.md](./archetypes.md) for the full taxonomy.

### Brittle Assumptions
The one or two critical dependencies in every investment thesis that, 
if invalidated, destroy the entire model. Systematically surfaced by 
AI Judgment Infrastructure before capital deployment rather than after.

### The Rigor Protocol
The organizational standard required to operationalize AI Judgment 
Infrastructure at institutional scale. A commitment to evaluating 
investment decisions by structural defense against adversarial models, 
rather than narrative persuasion.
